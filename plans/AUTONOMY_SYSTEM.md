# Autonomy System — Design & Implementation Plan

**Feature:** Character Agency & Ambitions  
**Phase:** Phase B of the Autonomous Character Agency System  
**Status:** Planned  
**Prerequisite:** `OPINIONS_SYSTEM.md` (Phase A) must be implemented first  
**Companion doc:** `OPINIONS_SYSTEM.md`

---

## Design Philosophy

The player acts as **a hand at the stern, not a director**. Characters have wants, cultural obligations, and social drives. The player can shape conditions — who lives near whom, what buildings exist, what culture the settlement drifts toward — but cannot simply instruct people to form relationships or perform cultural rites they aren't ready for.

Characters pursue goals through the event system. Most autonomous actions surface as events the player can accept, negotiate, or block (usually at a cost). Small background changes — opinion drift, cultural pull on ambition formation — happen silently. Nothing significant happens without player awareness.

The design is inspired by Crusader Kings 3's character agency: characters have persistent desires that build over time and translate into narrative moments. Unlike CK3, the settlement is small enough that every individual matters, so ambitions are modest and personal rather than dynastic.

---

## Core Concept: `PersonAmbition`

Every person can hold at most one active ambition at a time. Ambitions are generated by the simulation when conditions align — they aren't assigned manually. Once an ambition generates an event, the player responds. Once resolved (fulfilled or failed), the ambition is cleared and a new one may form over the following turns.

Ambitions are **visible** in PersonDetail as a small badge. The player can see what characters want. They cannot suppress or override it — the simulation will keep regenerating similar ambitions if the underlying conditions persist.

## Data Model

### `PersonAmbition` — added to `src/simulation/population/person.ts`

```typescript
export type AmbitionId =
  | 'seek_spouse'          // Unmarried adult wants to marry someone they like
  | 'seek_council'         // High-skill person wants a council seat
  | 'seek_seniority'       // Wife wants to become senior_wife
  | 'seek_cultural_duty'   // Young Sauromatian male wants to perform keth-thara
  | 'seek_informal_union'; // Imanian man wants to formalise a concubine relationship

export interface PersonAmbition {
  id: AmbitionId;
  targetPersonId?: string; // for seek_spouse and seek_informal_union
  intensity: number;       // 0.0–1.0; increases each turn the condition holds
  turnCreated: number;
}
```

**On `Person` interface:**

```typescript
ambition: PersonAmbition | null;
```

**Defaults:**
- `createPerson()` sets `ambition: null`
- `deserializePerson()` uses `?? null` for old saves
- Plain object — no Map serialisation needed; JSON round-trips cleanly

## Ambition Generation Rules

Evaluated every **8 turns** per person in `processDawn()`. Checked in priority order — first match wins.

| Ambition | Generation condition |
|---|---|
| `seek_spouse` | Unmarried, age ≥ 18, has opinion ≥ 40 of any eligible marriage partner *(opposite sex, appropriate age, no shared parents, spouse slot available under their cultural rules)* |
| `seek_council` | Not on council, in settlement ≥ 4 turns, leadership OR diplomacy skill ≥ 55 (Good–Very Good) |
| `seek_seniority` | `householdRole === 'wife'`, household has ≥ 3 wives, opinion of current `seniorWifeId` < 0 |
| `seek_cultural_duty` | Male, age 16–24, `primaryCulture` in `SAUROMATIAN_CULTURE_IDS`, no prior keth-thara duty in `eventHistory` |
| `seek_informal_union` | Male, `primaryCulture` Imanian (concubine rules allow), opinion ≥ 50 of an unmarried adult woman already in the settlement |

If no condition matches, `ambition` stays null. Ambitions are not generated for people with `role === 'away'` or `socialStatus === 'thrall'`.

**Intensity drift:** Each turn an ambition is held without resolution, intensity increases by `+0.05` (capped at 1.0). An ambition at intensity ≥ 0.7 becomes eligible to fire its event. This means a newly formed ambition waits ~3 turns before generating an event, giving the player a brief notice period via the badge.

---

## Ambition Evaluation

Checked the same tick as generation. If status is `'fulfilled'` or `'failed'`, the ambition is cleared.

| Ambition | Fulfilled | Failed |
|---|---|---|
| `seek_spouse` | Person is now married | Target is dead, departed, or no longer eligible |
| `seek_council` | Person is now on the council | No council seats available for ≥ 8 turns after formation |
| `seek_seniority` | Person now has `householdRole === 'senior_wife'` | Household dissolved; person no longer a wife |
| `seek_cultural_duty` | Person now has `role === 'keth_thara'` | Person is now > 24 years old |
| `seek_informal_union` | Target is now in person's household as a concubine | Target marries someone else or leaves |

### `evt_mutual_attraction` — "An Understanding"

**Fires when:** Two unmarried people have mutual opinion ≥ 70, and at least one has a `seek_spouse` ambition targeting the other.

> *The settlement has noticed what you may have missed. [subject.first] and [beloved.first] are rarely apart, finishing each other's sentences across the fire. It would be a match they would both welcome.*

| Choice | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Bless the union** | `performMarriage(subject, beloved)` — both ambitions cleared; +10 opinion from all who share culture with either party |
| **Encourage patience** *(cooldown 4 turns)* | No change. Event can re-fire when cooldown expires. Ambitions persist and intensity continues to build. |
| **Forbid it** | −20 opinion from both toward player-proxy (reflected as `modify_standing −5`) ; ambitions cleared; event will not re-fire unless new opinions form |

**Actor requirements:**
- `subject` — unmarried, age ≥ 18, has `seek_spouse` ambition
- `beloved` — unmarried, age ≥ 18, opposite sex to `subject`, opinion of `subject` ≥ 70

---

### `evt_informal_union_proposed` — "A Private Request"

**Fires when:** An Imanian man holds `seek_informal_union` ambition with intensity ≥ 0.7.

> *[petitioner.first] has come to you privately. He speaks well of [target.first] and asks whether you would recognise her as his companion under Imanian custom. The decision, he says, is ultimately yours.*

| Choice | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Approve the union** | `formConcubineRelationship(petitioner, target, 'imanian_concubine', state)` — ambition cleared; +5 standing |
| **Decline for now** *(cooldown 6 turns)* | Ambition clears. −5 opinion of player-proxy from petitioner. Event will not re-fire unless ambition regenerates. |
| **Refuse entirely** | Ambition clears. −10 opinion of player-proxy. All Imanian-primary-culture settlers lose −3 opinion of each other (community disappointment in your judgment). |

**Actor requirements:**
- `petitioner` — male, Imanian primary culture, has `seek_informal_union` ambition
- `target` — female, unmarried, adult, already in settlement

---

### `evt_hearth_companion_request` — "A Bond the Women Ask You to Name"

**Fires when:** Two women share a Sauromatian household, have mutual opinion ≥ 65, have lived together for ≥ 6 turns (tracked by `household.foundedTurn`), and no ashka-melathi bond already exists between them.

> *The women of [household name] have come to you together. [bondedA.first] and [bondedB.first] do not ask for ceremony or rank — only that you recognise what already is. Among their people, this bond has a name: ashka-melathi.*

| Choice | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Acknowledge the bond** | Add `[bondedA.id, bondedB.id]` to `household.ashkaMelathiBonds`; +10 opinion for all Sauromatian-culture persons in settlement; fire `hh_ashka_melathi_deepens` (deferred 2 turns) |
| **Defer the question** *(cooldown 6 turns)* | No change. Event can re-fire. |
| **Decline to recognise it** | −15 opinion of all Sauromatian-culture persons toward player-proxy; both women lose −10 opinion of each other (shame damages what was unnamed) |

**Actor requirements:**
- `bondedA` — female, `householdRole` senior_wife or wife, Sauromatian primary culture
- `bondedB` — female, `householdRole` wife or concubine, same household as `bondedA`

---

### `evt_council_petition` — "They Ask for a Voice"

**Fires when:** A person has a `seek_council` ambition with intensity ≥ 0.7.

> *[petitioner.first] approaches when others have left. They do not complain. They simply say: 'I have been here long enough to have earned a seat. You know what I can do. My counsel is yours, if you want it.'*

| Choice | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Grant a council seat** *(requires an open seat)* | `addCouncilMember(petitioner.id)` — ambition cleared; +15 opinion from all who know petitioner |
| **Acknowledge their service, offer a role instead** | Set `petitioner.role` to a specialist role matching their highest skill (healer, guard, trader, craftsman); ambition cleared; +5 opinion from petitioner toward player-proxy |
| **Decline respectfully** | Ambition clears. −15 opinion from petitioner + all persons who share their primary culture, toward the council members they know |

**Actor requirements:**
- `petitioner` — not currently on council, leadership or diplomacy ≥ 55, in settlement ≥ 4 turns

---

### `evt_keth_thara_selfvow` — "He Comes in the Old Way"

**Fires when:** A young Sauromatian male holds `seek_cultural_duty` ambition with intensity ≥ 0.7.

> *[subject.first] comes to you at dawn, dressed simply, carrying nothing he wasn't born with. He speaks the old words your Sauromatian settlers have translated for you. He is asking to go. Not as punishment. Not as exile. As the thing his culture asks of young men.*

| Choice | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| **Accept — send him on keth-thara** | `assignKethThara(subject.id)` — ambition fulfilled; +15 opinion of all Sauromatian-culture persons toward player-proxy |
| **Ask him to wait a season** *(available once; cooldown 4 turns)* | Intensity continues to build. Event re-fires next eligible turn. |
| **Forbid it** | Ambition clears. −20 opinion from subject toward player-proxy. All Sauromatian-culture women in settlement lose −8 opinion of player-proxy. The obligation is culturally unresolved and *will not appear again for the same person* — a permanent wound in the relationship. |

**Actor requirements:**
- `subject` — male, age 16–24, `primaryCulture` in `SAUROMATIAN_CULTURE_IDS`, role not `away` or `keth_thara`

## New Prerequisite Type

```typescript
// engine.ts — added to EventPrerequisite union
{ type: 'has_person_with_ambition'; ambitionId: AmbitionId }
```

Evaluated in `event-filter.ts`: returns true if any living, non-thrall, non-away person in `state.people` has `ambition?.id === ambitionId` and `ambition.intensity >= 0.7` (events don't fire on newly formed ambitions).

## Scope Boundaries

**Included in Phase B:**
- 5 ambition types with full generation/evaluation/tick logic
- 5 autonomous events + integration into `relationships.ts`
- `has_person_with_ambition` prerequisite type
- PersonDetail ambition badge with intensity hint

**Excluded from Phase B (future work):**
- Factional allegiances or loyalty to external tribes
- Religious conviction depth (lives in Religion System plan)
- Global fame / settlement-wide notoriety stat
- CK3-style intrigue (blackmail, threatened exposure, murder plots)
- Departure as an explicit ambition — departure is the consequence of sustained low opinions, handled by `evt_settler_departure` in Phase A
- Ambitions for children / minors — adults only
- Multiple simultaneous ambitions per person — one at a time, always

## Open Questions / Future Work

1. **Ambition conflict** — What happens when two people have mutually exclusive ambitions (both `seek_seniority` in the same household)? Currently they'd both generate events independently; a future `evt_seniority_rivalry` could resolve the conflict in a single event.

2. **Player-directed ambition** — Should the player ever be able to nudge a character toward an ambition? E.g., deliberately placing two people near each other, or assigning someone to a teaching role, could lower the generation threshold. This is subtle (environmental, not direct) and fits the "hand at the stern" model.

3. **Ambition failure narrative** — Currently failed ambitions clear silently. A future `evt_ambition_abandoned` could fire when ambition fails (intensity ≥ 0.6 + fails), giving the person a small negative mood modifier rather than no consequence.

4. **Tribe-origin loyalty** — Sauromatian founding women may develop a `seek_return` ambition if settlement opinion drops low enough. This bridges the Opinions system departure event with the tribal relationship system in a future phase.
